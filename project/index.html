Embracing Ancient warfare armoury Induku.

Dating back to the period during Shaka Zulu’s reign as king of Zulus’ ,Zulu warriors made use of induku,ubhoko ,umsila and ihawu to prepare for and to fight wars.

Shaka Zulu himself, was a excellent stick fighter by the age of 11.

Fast forward to today ,young Zulu men use the very same weaponry including the INDUKU for competitive stick fighting at festivals, including weddings just for entertainment.

I personally choose this project for creating the INDUKU enabled me to learn a couple of carving skills.

About INDUKUAbout

Since ancient times men would own their personal fighting sticks ,one of them being known as INDUKU.A young man may acquire his INDUKU by the age of 16.


Induku is a stick fighting weapon used for striking an opponent. alongside the INDUKU the fighter will also use the Ubhoko, used for body protection and the Umsila and Ihawu used to protect the knuckles.Today however we will focus on the INDUKU only.


Induku is a strong stick or shaft of wood . The stick is carved smooth using various sharp tools such as saws, knives or axes and it is used specifically for stick fighting. Induku is about 88 centimetres long. A piece of cowhide or any other preferable material may be tied around one end of the stick to ensure the fighter’s grip on the weapon.
